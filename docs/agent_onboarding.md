# Agent Onboarding

## Agent Role

Agents support a telecom campaign by contacting prioritized customers, confirming customer needs, explaining relevant options, and logging clean CRM notes.

## First-Day Setup

- Review the campaign playbook.
- Understand the three campaign motions: retention, upgrade, renewal.
- Read the top customer segments.
- Practice the opening lines and objection responses.
- Confirm CRM fields required after each contact.

## Call Flow

1. Confirm identity and ask if it is a good time.
2. Explain why the customer was selected.
3. Ask one context question.
4. Match the customer to the right offer or follow-up.
5. Confirm the next action.
6. Log outcome, sentiment, objection, and follow-up date.

## Objection Handling

### "It is too expensive."

Ask which cost matters most: monthly fee, installation, bundle cost, or contract length. Offer a comparison only after the customer confirms the concern.

### "The service has been unreliable."

Acknowledge the issue first. Review the support-ticket history and offer escalation or service-quality review before discussing renewal.

### "I need to think about it."

Set a specific follow-up date and send a short summary through the preferred channel.

### "I already have another provider."

Ask what the new provider solves better. Capture competitive feedback for the manager report.

## CRM Notes

Every record should include:

- Contact outcome.
- Main customer need.
- Primary objection.
- Sentiment.
- Offer discussed.
- Follow-up date.
- Agent recommendation.
